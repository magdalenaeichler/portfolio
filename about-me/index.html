I'm Magdalena Eichler and I'm an aspiring UX designer.

I have some background in graphic design creating promotional materials for my company and previous jobs. I've prepared tones of WordPress event pages which gave me an interesting experience.

All of this led me to the path I want to follow: to be a part of the process of creating the best possible user experience with digital interfaces.

Now I'm open to new opportunities!

I'm always open to new tools

Learning Figma was fun. Creating an interactive prototype and giving it to potential users to test without coding them was an experience. As a self-taught Jira administrator in my small company, I had difficult moments, but I always say that nothing is impossible we just don't have enough time.

Tools I know:
  • Figma - quite good! I can prepare something interactive
  • Adobe Creative Cloud:
    • XD - basics: I used it in one project
    • Illustrator - not master, but I'm ok with it
    • Photoshop - basics: I can remove zits from the face and few more things
    • InDesign - basics: posters, leaflets, short e-books
  • Jira - let's say we're not enemies
  • Trello - I like this tool!

I'm curious and I like to go deep

I love learning new things! If I do something for pandas, I will find scientific articles about pandas abilities to distinguish between colours and zoologists reports about their natural habitats.

I want to know people (creatures?) I'm designing for, who they are, what they desire, what they thought. How scientists describe the group I'm learning about, what researchers found. I want to know as much as possible.

It's impossible to know everything. But there is a fantastic way to learn more - testing created things. I'm not afraid of showing cardboard prototypes. It's fast and useful!

I'm quite good at:
  • research - information seeking, online and offline (I'm not afraid of a library)
  • learning new things
  • writing articles (in Polish)
  • sketching squares and lines on the cardboard

I've coded this page by myself - static page in HTML and CSS, based on Bootstrap 4 files. In the past, I also started to learn programming languages: PHP, Java, JavaScript and the last one I liked the most.

My future goal is to become a unicorn - UX, UI and front-end developer in one person.

I know:
